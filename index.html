Life Story Stuff

There’s a little island off the coast of nowhere in particular, where, for reasons no one has ever fully explained, goats outnumber people by about 15 to 1. I visited this island during what I call my “wanderlust phase,” but honestly, I was just bad at booking flights and ended up on the wrong plane. When I arrived, I was greeted by a friendly local named Hugo, who insisted that I stay in his family’s ancient stone house because “tourists never come here.” Hugo’s house was charming, though I could never quite shake the feeling that the goats were watching me. One morning, I woke up to find the goats had broken into the yard and were now solemnly chewing on my socks, which I had hung to dry outside. I spent hours trying to retrieve them, running around like a lunatic while the goats effortlessly outsmarted me. By the end of the day, I was sockless, exhausted, and oddly contemplative about the nature of resilience. Hugo, seeing my plight, decided to cheer me up with a local delicacy: a mysterious dish he called "caprine delight." I won’t get into the details, but let’s just say it involved a lot of yogurt and a suspiciously chewy texture. I politely nibbled and then excused myself, wondering if my wanderlust phase had officially gone too far. What does this have to do with cookies, you ask? Nothing. Absolutely nothing. Except that when I finally got home, I found myself baking cookies

Step 1

First of all you need to gather your ingredients and it usually helps putting all of them on a table instead of having to get the additional ones everytime you move onto the next step. Trust me I would know.

Step 2

After you gathered everything make sure to weigh them before throwing them into the bowl. Now weigh and toss in the vegan butter, powdered sugar, vanilla sugar, salt and lastly flour

Step 3

Get to kneading and keep kneading until it feels slightly wet and looks like a proper dough

Step 4

Now let the dough rest for 30 minutes, he deserves it

Step 5

After his well deserved rest, get a plastic bag and cut/rip it half. Put your well rested dough inbetween the two plastic sheets and start going to town on it with a rolling pin. Roll until it feels thin enough. Afterwards use a cookie cutter (shape is irrelevant) to cut the cookies

Step 6

Migrate your freshly cut out cookies onto baking foils and let them rest for another hour. Also start pre-heating the oven to 200 degree Celsius.

Step 7

Put your cookies in the middle of your pre-heated oven and wait around 6 minutes for them to be baked. They should be slighty brown but don't overdo it.

Step 8

Put the fresh cookies onto a grid and let them cool for a bit. Now you can either eat your cookies or smear a combination of Raspberry jam, Carob jelly and powdered sugar inbetween two cookies.
